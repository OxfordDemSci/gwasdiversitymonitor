About

This interactive dashboard monitors the diversity of participants across all published Genome Wide Association Studies (GWAS), the primary technique used for genetic discovery. The objective is to identify statistical associations between a set of genetic variants across different individuals (Single Nucleotide Polymorphisms, or 'SNPs') with specific traits of interest. This monitor represents an extension of our earlier project, 'The Scientometrics of Genome Wide Association Studies' published in Communications Biology in January 2019. As there, we leverage the magnificent dataset curated by the NHGRI-EBI Catalog (subject to their licensing information which can be found here). The dashboard itself is a combination of Python (Flask) and JavaScript (D3), designed to be used in modern web browsers for presentation. An earlier prototype appears here, with the full code base available for replication on GitHub. We are actively encouraging community-based suggestions and contributions. The backend code checks daily for updates to the GWAS Catalog, writing to logs and refreshing the dataset which powers the dashboard as appropriate.

The dashboard is under constant development and review, but is currently comprised of two global widgets: METRIC (which toggles whether we are evaluating by number of sutdies or by number of participants), and STAGE (which determines whether we are considering the discovery or replication phase of research). Local widgets also toggle the 'EFO Parent Term', the 'Broader' ancestry category, and 'Year' (related to year of study). Due to the relatively small size of the dataset, not all widgets apply to each figure, and we describe our design choices below:

  • Summary Breakdown: Total GWAS participants diversity. This part displays summary statistics without any filtering (over time, ancestry, traits, or otherwise)
  • Bubble Plot: Ancestry over time by parent term. This provides a granular overview of all GWAS in the Catalog, mapping onto EFO Parent terms. In addition to being affected by the STAGE global widget, it also allows a finer, more granular search term based on individual EFO Traits (and combinations of).
  • Time Series Plot: Participants across all parent terms. This figure displays how 'Broader Ancestry' varies over time across the two global widgets. Note: due to the fact that that lesser studied ancestries are rarely used to study specific parent terms, we do not utilize the 'Parent Term' widget here. The tickbox for 'Include not recorded' provides a robustness check with regards to how we are mapping our 'Broader Ancestry' field.
  • Heatmap: Parent term by 'broader' ancestry. This figure displays how many studies are done across both STAGE and METRIC, broken down into each of the individual 'Broader Ancestry' and 'Parent Terms' across each of the years in the dataset.
  • Choropleth Map. Due to the sparsity of countries recruited from across all EFO Parent terms, this figure draws from the two global widgets, adjustable by Year only. Polygon data from here.
  • Doughnut Chart. This figure displays the percent of which 'Broader Ancestry' is used to study which 'Parent Term' across both the global widgets. A 'Show'\'Hide' toggle allows an inset which also shows the breakdown of associations discovered across all EFO Parent terms at the discovery stage alone.
